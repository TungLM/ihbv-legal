Privacy Policy for iHBV

Effective date: May 17, 2026

iHBV is a hepatitis B tracking app published by TM Tech Labs. This policy explains how iHBV handles personal data, sensitive data, health data, and device data.

Summary

iHBV is designed to store your health tracking information on your device. The current release does not require an account, does not show ads, does not sell user data, and does not use third-party analytics or advertising SDKs.

Information You Enter

Depending on how you use the app, iHBV may let you enter or store:

  • Medication names, medication schedules, reminder times, and medication history.
  • Follow-up appointments and related notes.
  • Lab values or health records such as HBV DNA, ALT, AST, and care notes.
  • App settings such as privacy mode, app lock, and reminder preferences.

This information may be sensitive health data.

How Data Is Stored

In the current release, health tracking data is stored locally on your device. The local database is encrypted on device storage. iHBV does not upload your health records to a cloud server in the current release.

If a later version adds cloud sync, account sign-in, backup, or cross-device sync, this policy and the Google Play Data safety disclosure must be updated before release.

How We Use Information

iHBV uses information you enter to:

  • Show medication schedules, follow-up appointments, and health tracking history.
  • Schedule local medication and follow-up reminders.
  • Show trends and summaries based on data you entered.
  • Apply privacy controls, such as hiding sensitive data on screen or in notifications.

We do not use your health data for advertising, advertising profiles, or sale to third parties.

Data Sharing

The current release does not share your health records with TM Tech Labs, advertisers, data brokers, or other third parties.

iHBV may use operating system services such as local notifications, biometric authentication or app lock, secure storage, and encrypted local database storage to provide app features. These services operate on your device.

Device Permissions

iHBV may request or use the following permissions:

  • Notifications: used to show medication and follow-up reminders.
  • Biometrics or fingerprint: used for the optional app lock feature if enabled on your device.

iHBV should not request permissions that are unnecessary for the submitted store release. If a later version adds Health Connect, HealthKit, location, camera, microphone, files, payments, or cloud services, app permissions, in-app notices, store declarations, and this policy must be updated.

Retention and Deletion

Because the current release stores data locally on your device, you control that data through the app and device. You can delete records in the app when deletion features are available, clear app data in device settings, or uninstall the app to remove local data from that device.

If a later version adds accounts or cloud features, we will provide instructions for deleting server-side data.

Security

iHBV uses local database encryption and in-app privacy controls to help protect sensitive health information. No app or storage method can guarantee absolute security, so you should protect your device with a strong passcode, biometric lock, and operating system updates.

Children

iHBV is not directed to children under 13. The app is intended for adults and caregivers who track hepatitis B information. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

If you believe a child under 13 has provided personal information through iHBV, please contact us so we can review and respond.

Medical Disclaimer

iHBV is a tracking and reminder tool. The app does not diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent disease, and it does not replace professional medical advice. Always consult a qualified healthcare professional when making medical decisions.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy when app features, data handling practices, laws, or store requirements change. Updates will include a new effective date.
